The Case For Setup B
+ 2× capacity for ~$579K more — grain handling & bottling nearly identical in both quotes.
+ Lower fully-loaded cost per bottle — same fixed overhead spread over 2× the bottles ($8.26 vs $9.29).
+ Contract distilling ready — unused capacity generates revenue while bourbon ages.
+ More jobs created — aligns with city partnership and community investment goals.
+ 7+ years of growth headroom. Setup A could max out by Year 3–4.
+ Setup B at 40% utilization = Setup A at 80% output.
+ $579K equipment premium pays for itself vs. $1.38M cost of adding a second shift to Setup A over 5 years.
Things to Plan For
− Higher startup cost (~$579K more) — requires more capital or a larger financing package.
− Higher base monthly OpEx ($85K vs $75K) — slightly more overhead to carry in early years.
− More bourbon aging capital tied up — 2× production = 2× barrels in storage for 3+ years.
− Year 1 & 2 profit lower than Setup A at equivalent sell-through due to higher OpEx — Setup B catches up at Year 3+.
